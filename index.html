Tamago sushi
たまご寿司
Ingredients:
  • Egg
  • Mirin (rice wine)
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Combine water (2 Tbsp), sugar, mirin, salt, and rice vinegar (1/4 tsp). Mix until sugar is dissolved. Add eggs, and mix very well.
  • Step 2: Heat a pan at medium temperature and add oild. Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture in the pan, tilting to cover the bottom of the pan.
  • Step 3: Repeat adding egg to the pan and rolling back and forth until the egg is used up. Remove from the pan and cool completely.
  • Step 4: Cut the egg log in half. Then slice 1/4" thick vertical slices. Cut Nori into 5" x 1/3" strips
  • Step 5: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar. Make sushi ball and top each rice ball with a slice of egg, and wrap around with Nori strips.
Hirame sushi
ひらめ寿司
Ingredients:
  • Hirame fillet
  • Sushi Rice/Sushi Vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: First, descale the fish. This will be difficult as the scales are fine and numerous. After, wash it thoroughly.
  • Step 2: At the pectoral fin, make a small cut on both sides and cut along the head. Then, slit the belly and remove the entrails by scraping them out, along with the dark blood.
  • Step 3: Rinse, and cut along the fin. Remove the bones along the belly by slicing them off at a right angle to the fish. Remove the fins as well.
  • Step 4: Cut the fillet into 1/10 inch (3mm) slices by slanting the knife to the right and starting from the left.
  • Step 5: Cook sushi rice and add sushi vinegar to it. Put Hirame on sushi rice and serve as you like
Hamachi sushi
はまち寿司
Ingredients:
  • Hamachi fillet
  • Shiso cress leaves
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Slice the hamachi into thin strips using a very sharp knife.
  • Step 2: Cook sushi rice. Place the vinegar, salt and sugar in a large bowl and mix well. Microwave the mixture for 10 seconds or until warmed through. Then mix the rice with sushi vinegar.
  • Step 3: To assemble the sushi, measure out 10 portions of sushi rice, each weighing 12g. Using the palm of your hand, form the rice balls into even sized oblongs and place on a metal tray.
  • Step 4: Lay the hamachi slices on top of the rice and sear with a blowtorch until lightly charred. Once seared, divide the nigiri evenly between serving dishes
  • Step 5: To serve, garnish each nigiri with a shiso cress leaf. Place a little sushi ginger and wasabi on each plate and serve immediately
Ebi sushi
えび寿司
Ingredients:
  • Black tiger shrimp
  • Bamboo skewers
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Using your thumb on the spine and your fingers on the feet, straighten out the shrimp. Take a skewer and pierce the shrimp from head to tail.
  • Step 2: Boil the shrimp for about 60 – 90 seconds. You want the shrimp to feel firm.
  • Step 3: After the shrimp have turned a nice orange/white color and have become firm, take them out and put them into an ice bath (Ice and water in a bowl).
  • Step 4: Once the shrimp are cold, peel off the shells and cut the shrimp from head to tail, making sure to only cut about half way down.
  • Step 5: Put the shrimp on top of cooked sushi rice and serve as you like.
Hokkigai sushi
ほっきがい寿司
Ingredients:
  • Surf clam
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Holding the clam firmly, insert the tip of the knife and sever the muscle holding the shell together.
  • Step 2: Cut the muscles holding the shell halves together. Put the shelled clams in a colander placed in a bowl, and rinse 3 times.
  • Step 3: Slice in half horizontally. Make sure that you retain a bit of the black guts as you rinse the clams. This black part has a lot of flavor and umami, so I recommend that you don't take it all off.
  • Step 4: Wash the clams one by one while rubbing them with your hands. Change the water at least 10 times as you wash them. The clams are ready when there is no more sand in the bowl.
  • Step 5: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar. Make sushi ball and top each rice ball with a slice of egg, and wrap around with Nori strips.
Tako sushi
だから寿司
Ingredients:
  • Frozen octopus
  • Kombu (Japanese kelp)
  • Daikon (Japanese radish)
  • Sushi Rice/Sushi Vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Put on a pot of water, about a gallon, and add a good sized piece of kombu and about 2 oz of sea salt. Bring to a boil.
  • Step 2: Thaw octopus in cold water, then drain well.
  • Step 3: Knead the octopus with fresh grated/chopped daikon radish and sea salt. While doing that, turn the head inside out and check for any leftover viscera. Remove any that’s found, then return the head sac to it’s correct shape.
  • Step 4: Dunk the octopus into the boiling water a few times until the tentacles curl up. Lower the heat a bit, and then simmer the octopus for about 5-10 minutes.
  • Step 5: Let the octopus cool in the liquid for at least an hour. Remove the octopus from the water and let it dry a bit. Slice on an angle, then serve with Sushi rice as you like.
Saba sushi
さば寿司
Ingredients:
  • Fish's worth fresh mackerel
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Rub the fillets with sugar, place in a tray, cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 2 hours. Rinse off the fillets. Rub salt and cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 3 hours.
  • Step 2: Rinse off the fillets. Pour the vinegar into the shallow tray with the fillets placed skin side down. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 4 hours.
  • Step 3: Peel off the skin. Remove bones and slice off a bit of the fish in the middle that is thicker than the rest.
  • Step 4: Line a sushi rolling mat with plastic wrap, and place a mackerel fillet on it skin side down. Cover evenly with sushi rice, and roll with the mat.
  • Step 5: Secure the mat with elastic bands, leave for 2 hours and cut it into pieces.
Kani sushi
カニ寿司
Ingredients:
  • Kanikama sticks
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Cook Kanikama stick and cut each stick into smaller pieces (about 2 inches)
  • Step 2: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar. Make sushi rice ball out of it.
  • Step 3: Place the kanikama piece on the rice, and then press it firmly to stick onto the rice. You can use one hand to hold the nigiri sushi, and use the hand hand to press. (Optional: you can use a small portion of wasabi to “glue” the crab stick to the rice for a more steady nigiri sushi.)
  • Step 4: Cut the seaweed into 1/4- inch strips and wrap each strip around the width of the nigiri to secure the crab stick to the rice.
  • Step 5: Place the assembled nigiri on the plate. Serve and enjoy the kani suchi!
Anago sushi
アナゴ寿司
Ingredients:
  • Anago
  • Mirin
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar/Soy Sauce
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Prepare Tsume(Eel Sauce) by repeated boiling of Anago and keep the sauce to cool.
  • Step 2: Take the balance eel, brush the flesh with Tsume and broil for 2 minutes. Repeat it for another 2 times and bring it to room temperature.
  • Step 3: Arrange the eel, flesh down and skin side up, on the bottom of the mold or pan. Trim and patch the strip of eel, if necessary, to cover the bottom entirely.
  • Step 4: Paint the top of each piece with a bit of sauce.
  • Step 5: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar. Make sushi ball and top each rice ball with a slice of Anago, and wrap around with Nori strips. Serve with pink or red pickled ginger and wasabi if you like.
Ikura sushi
イクラ寿司
Ingredients:
  • Mirin
  • Cured salmon roe
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar/Soy Sauce/Wasabi
Recipe:
  • Step 1: In a medium bowl, combine salmon roe with soy sauce, mirin or sake, and dashi. Let stand for at least 15 and up to 30 minutes. (This is a good time to cook the rice.) If you can find Japanese cured salmon roe at a Japanese market, you can use it as is, without the marination step. Otherwise, salt-cured salmon roe (sold by good fishmongers) will work with the marination step listed here.
  • Step 2: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar.
  • Step 3: Make sushi ball and lace a small amount of wasabi on each rice ball.
  • Step 4: Cut a piece of nori 5cm by 10cm and wrap around the rice ball so that there is a rim to hold in the salmon roe.
  • Step 5: Top the rice with 20g salmon roe and enjoy!
Futomaki sushi
太巻き寿司
Ingredients:
  • Dashimaki tamago
  • Cooked unagi (freshwater eel)
  • Vege: Simmered shiitake mushrooms/kanpyo/Spinach/Cucumber
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar/Sushi sakura denbu
  • Water/Salt/Sugar
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Boil all the vegetables and make sure they are spliced into stick shape. Gather all the ingredients.
  • Step 2: Place a bamboo sushi mat on the working surface or cutting board. Then place a sheet of nori on the bamboo mat, shiny side down. Divide sushi rice into quarters.
  • Step 3: Leaving about a ½ inch (1.5 cm) strip along the top of nori farthest away from you.
  • Step 4: Place the cucumber toward the bottom of nori. Front ingredients will need to go over the other ingredients.
  • Step 5: From the bottom end (of sushi rice), start rolling nori sheet over the filling tightly and firmly with bamboo mat until the bottom end reaches the end of sushi rice on top. Use your fingers to hold the ingredients when you roll.
Philadelphia sushi
フィラデルフィア寿司
Ingredients:
  • Smoked salmon
  • Small cucumber (cut into matchsticks)
  • Nori (dried edible seaweed)
  • Sushi rice/Sushi vinegar
  • Water/Salt/Sugar/Cream cheese
Recipe:
  • Step 1: Cook sushi rice and mix it with vinegar. On the rolling mat place one sheet of nori with the rough side facing upwards.
  • Step 2: Wet your hands and grab a handful of rice and place it on the nori. Spread the rice evenly throughout the nori without smushing the rice down.
  • Step 3: Arrange, in a horizontal row 1 inch (2 cm) from the bottom, smoked salmon, cream cheese, and cucumber.
  • Step 4: Grabbing both nori and the mat, roll the mat over the filling so the extra space at the bottom touches the other side, squeezing down to make a nice tight roll. Squeeze down along the way to keep the roll from holding its shape.
  • Step 5: Transfer the roll onto a cutting board. Rub a knife on a damp paper towel before slicing the roll into six equal portions.
